Avani

Food

Azul Cocina Artesanal & Cantina

Best Mexican food and ambiance — sit in the outdoor garden.

Corazon Comedor

Second choice — even better food, but very little ambiance.

In-N-Out

Your kids may not be aware of the secret menu, including the 3x3, 4x4, and Animal Style.

McConnell's or Rori's

The best ice cream options — both are local and have several locations around the area.

Santa Barbara Public Market

If you need a place with lots of options to satisfy lots of people, the food court is wonderful — and they have a Rori's ice cream there too.

Activities

Santa Barbara County Courthouse

Beautiful, and there are decent docent-led tours.

Sea Center (on the wharf)

Very small, good for a five year old. Could do 30 minutes to an hour.

MOXI

A fun, hands-on museum downtown — but you can find a similar hands-on museum experience almost anywhere.

Santa Barbara Zoo

Very cute, but probably not great for older kids.

Physical Activities

Kayaking

Recommend Paddle Sports Center — kayak under the catamaran on your way out.

Short hikes

Short hikes are all over the place — here's a list of easy trails around the area.

Steps down to the beach

There are two spots where you can hike down steps to the beach: Mesa Lane Steps and 1000 Steps Beach. Parking for either may require finding a spot and walking.

Waterfall hikes

For more rigorous hikes to waterfalls, try the Seven Falls hike or the San Ysidro Falls hike. Both are 3–4 miles roundtrip and take some time, but they're beautiful.

Bike rentals

Tourist bike rentals are available along the beach.

Walk State Street

Walk up and down State Street. Don't miss the turtle fountain in La Arcada, a very cute little shopping arcade at State and Figueroa (my eye doctor is there ;)).

Alice Keck Park Memorial Gardens

Beautiful, with lots of ducks and turtles.
